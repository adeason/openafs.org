A Revolution in Filesystems

AFS is a distributed filesystem pioneered at Carnegie Mellon University. It offers a client-server architecture for federated file sharing and replicated read-only content distribution, providing location independence, scalability, security, and transparent migration capabilities. AFS is available for Unix, Linux, Mac, and Windows.
